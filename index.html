Wild aurochs

The aurochs is the ancestor of domestic cattle. It has been represented in many cave paintings.

  • Scientific name: Bos primigenius
  • Height at the shoulders: 155-180 cm for males; 135-155 cm for females.
  • Extinct: Last recorded in 1627.
  • Horns: Up to 80 cm long and 20 cm wide.

The aurochs was a large wild cattle, that lived in Europe, Asia and North Africa from the Pleistocene up to the 17th century when it went extinct. It is the ancestor of today's domesticated cattle.
